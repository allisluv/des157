Alli's DES 157 User Goals

How will your users understand the content of the project?

Because the Art of Collaboration app is meant for kids, it needs to cater to students of varying reading levels. It will be important that the navigation of the app relies heavily on visual language, and uses written lanuage as a back up.

It will also be important not to overload the user with information. the app should only ask one question at a time and provide intructions in a short and concise manner.

How will your users understand the primary objectives?

The app will encourage students to find other students to work with and share ideas with. The point of the app is to get student connected to art and each other, so the app will show users what others are working on.

What content and features do your users need, and in what form do they need it?

The most important feature of the app will be that it can upload and download images. This will allow students to shared both finished works and works in progress. It will also need a form that will guide users in putting together a portfolio. Finally, the app will have have a sketch pad that will allow users to create art even without other resources.

How do your users think your interactive projects should work and what are their experience levels with the type of media you are creating?

The users' experience with digital media will vary greatly. The Art of Collaboration app should be a good first experience with computers and tablets. The user will be able to navigate the app easily through clear visual language. Tablet touch screens will make navigation more instinctual for younger users with less computer experience.
